Manuscript Submission Guidelines
We invite researchers, academicians, industry professionals, and practitioners to submit original, high-quality, and unpublished research papers presenting conceptual, innovative, experimental, or theoretical contributions in the conference themes and related areas. Submission of a manuscript implies that the work has not been previously published and is not under consideration for publication elsewhere.
General Guidelines
	•	Manuscripts should be prepared according to the conference template and must be limited to 6–8 pages, including figures, tables, and references.
	•	All submitted papers will undergo a double-blind peer-review process and will be evaluated based on originality, technical quality, relevance, significance, and clarity of presentation.
	•	The corresponding author is responsible for ensuring that all co-authors have reviewed and approved the submitted manuscript and consented to its publication.
	•	Authors are solely responsible for the content of their submissions, including obtaining necessary permissions for copyrighted material.
	•	The conference organizers and publisher shall not be held liable for any claims arising from the content of submitted papers.
	•	Authors will be notified of the review outcome (acceptance, revision, or rejection) via email.
	•	At least one author of each accepted paper must complete the conference registration and present the paper at the conference for inclusion in the conference proceedings.
Guidelines for Oral Presentation
Authors of accepted papers are required to present their work during the conference. Each presentation will be allotted 15 minutes, including time for questions and discussion. You can use any form of presentation you wish. Please arrive for your presentation early to ensure the necessary arrangements.
